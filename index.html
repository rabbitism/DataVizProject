Olympics in a Nutshell

The modern Olympic Games are leading international sporting events featuring summer and winter sports competitions in which thousands of athletes from around the world participate in a variety of competitions. The Olympic Games are considered the world's foremost sports competition with more than 200 nations participating.

This page is an overview of all cities host modern Olympic Game in history, including both Sumer and Winter Olympic Games. Click on different tabs to check other topics.

Athletes in Olympics

Athletes participating in Olympic Games are increasing in over 100 years, and become steady in 21 century. This chart is devided into two parts. The top half is athletes in Summer Games, and the bottom half is atheletes in Winter Games.
Several interesting knowledge about this chart:

  • Olympic Game was cancelled twice due to world war
  • A decrease in 1956 and 1984, all of them are related to boycott against Soviet Union.
  • Winter Games was originally held in the same year with Summer Games. In 1994, it starts to held separately from Summer Games.

    • You can hover on bars to see detailed data in each year.

    Events in Olympics

    In the history of Olympics, different events in different sports keeps added or eliminated from the Game. In 1896 there are 14 nations competed in 43 events. And in latest Summer Game in Rio de Janeiro, atheltes from over 206 nations competed in 324 events. In latest Winter Game in Pyeongchang, there are 92 countries competed in 102 events.
